Megan Cole

Overview

Megan Cole is a 23 year old woman who is know for being a passionate runner, studious, and kind individual. Her childhood, education, and running career are all important aspects of her life that have helped mold her into the individual that she is today.

Quick Facts

  • Full name: Megan Leigh Cole
  • Born: March 27, 2001
  • Birthplace: Cincinnati, Ohio
  • Hometown: Versailles, Indiana

Early Life

Megan Cole grew up in a small Indiana town called Versailles. She lived with her parents, Jim and Karen Cole, and brother, Patrick. Growing up, Megan loved to play school and perform plays in their basement with her brother. She enjoyed playing outside and was always making a new craft. As a child, Megan's mom and father both returned to school to study to persue their passion of being teachers. With their busy days, Megan spent endless hours with her grandparents, Brenda and Larry Armbrecht. Megan developed a close relationship with them after endless hours of playing Uno and baking sugar cookies. Megan contributes a lot of her and her parent's success to the involvement that her grandparents had in her life. In addition, Megan's parents also contributed to her interests and encouraged her and Patrick to persue their dreams. Megan's father, Jim, was a cross country and track coach which led to Megan becoming interested in running. In third grade, she started doing Girls on the Run and enjoyed running with her friends. This led to a life-long hobby for her that also included fun, successful memories on the track.

Education

Megan attended South Ripley School coorporation from kindergarten to 12th grade. When Megan graduated from South Ripley High School, she aspired work in the medical field and decided to attend Northern Kentucky University to major in Biology. During her freshman year of college, Megan reliazed that this was not the career path she wanted to take. Megan decided to follow in her mother's footsteps and become an elementary school teacher. Megan student taught at Fort Thomas Independent schools and was excited to have her own classroom. She imagined herself being a 4th or 5th math or science grade teacher. Upon graduation from NKU in 2023, Megan accepted a 4th grade teaching position in Oldham County, Kentucky. However due to administration and staff changes, Megan ended up starting her teaching career teaching 1st grade. During her first year teaching, Megan was faced with several hardships with lack of administration support and poor behaviors in the classroom. Quickly, Megan realized that being a teacher was not her passion; she enjoyed creating lessons and making new resources, but the classroom management was very challenging for her. Megan felt very lost on what to do. During this time, her boyfriend, Ethan, began to teach her how to code in Python. Megan found this very fasinating and enjoyed learning how to build a program. Later, Megan learned about the field of EdTech and instantly became interested. She decided to persue a career in software engineering from Bellarmine University with the goal of becoming a software engineer for an EdTech company.

Running Career

In middle school, Megan began running cross country and track and field. Megan worked hard and broke two middle school track and field record in the 800 meter dash and 1600 meter run. This led to a successful transition into high school running. Undering the coaching of her dad, Megan broke the 5k cross country school record and 3200 track school record as a freshman. Throughout her high school cross country career, Megan qualified for 4 regionals, 4 semi-states, and qualified for the state meet as a senior in 2018. In track, Megan qualified for 4 regionals, 2 indoor state meets, and 2 outdoor state meets. In addition to her school's team, Megan also was apart of a running club, the Southeastern Indiana All-Stars. Megan attended several national meets and even earned 3 All-American Honors with the team. As her high school career wrapped up, Megan signed to run cross country and track at Northern Kentucky Univesity. For the Norse, Megan earned several team MVP rewards, broke several school records, and earned several All-Conference honors. After finishing up at NKU, Megan transitioned into casual running. In January 2024, Megan joined a competitive running team in Louisville, Kentucky called Swags Elite. Megan now enjoys spending her weekends running road races ranging from 5ks to half marathons
